Encode, Manage and Share Biomedical Knowledge Rapidly

The Knowledge Grid is an infrastructure that enables research findings to be converted from its original, human-readable format, into coded forms that can be leveraged by organizations or individuals to automate computation of health data.

Put Your Knowledge On The Grid

In my role as CEO of the “Heart Health Association”, I place the highest priority on developing evidence-based tools that both cardiologists and primary care docs can use in practice. My organization has led the development of several excellent predictive analytics and computable heart care toolkits. Thanks to the Knowledge Grid, it has been easy for us to create, manage, and update a collection of digital Knowledge Objects that carry the most current knowledge about heart health. We are delighted with the response. More people are taking advantage of the knowledge we have developed and organized than ever before.

-Miranda Grayson, MD, FACS

Deploy KNowledge Using The Grid

As Senior VP for Innovation for “Valiant”, I lead a technical team of 38 analysts, engineers, and programmers. Our job is to find ways to improve the Valiant Electronic Health Record (EHR) system. The Knowledge Grid has made it possible for us to rapidly add large amounts trusted, updated health knowledge from expert sources to the Va liant EHR. Every Valiant EHR user, and all the patients they assist, now benefit from pre dictions and guidelines that appear automatically on screen right when needed and in context. This includes the predictive analytics and toolkits from the “Heart Health Association”, which are deployed in the Valiant EHR and being used to help improve the health of thousa nds of people every day.

-Thomas S. Garcia, MBA

BENEFIT FROM KNOWLEDGE

Make Better Informed Health Decisions With The Grid

I am a primary care physician responsible for taking care of a large group of older adults. For years I have worked hard to keep up with the latest medical evidence but that is hard to do. My patients struggle with a great diversity of health issues. I used to struggle to provide my patients with the most current medical advice. However, a dramatic improvement has taken place. Today, the systems I use in the clinic provide me with medical evidence that is useful and tailored to each patient’s unique circumstances. The knowledge I receive now in the EHR is also prioritized for me and, when relevant, formatte d to make it easy for me to engage my patients in shared decision-making. I understand that these improvements are the result of a Knowledge Grid that moves knowledge everywhere it is needed in the health system. That’s amazing and appreciated.

-Charles Hawary, MD
