Story

There are three succeeding levels, each with a different user story. (Try the alt layout)

ADD KNOWLEDGE

Take actionable knowledge, make it machine-ready, and manage it.

In her role as CEO of the “Heart Health Association”, Tammy places the highest priority on developing evidence-based tools that both cardiologists and primary care docs can use in practice. Her organization has led the development of several excellent predictive analytics and computable heart care toolkits. Thanks to the Knowledge Grid, it has been easy for Tammy’s team to create, manage, and update a collection of digital knowledge objects that carry our most updated findings about heart health. She is delighted with the response. More people than ever are taking advantage of their knowledge, from IT systems to consumer apps and other health organizations. Moreover, the API backend automates computation of health data for third party applications, giving the “Heart Health Association” access to real-world usage data and a new source of income.

ACTIVATE KNOWLEDGE

Deploy the “added” knowledge to generate health advice.

As Senior VP for Innovation for “Valiant”, Miranda leads a technical team of analysts, engineers, and programmers. Their job is to find ways to improve the Valiant Electronic Health Record (EHR) system. The Knowledge Grid has made it possible for them to rapidly add large amounts of trusted, up-to-date, health knowledge from expert sources to the Valiant EHR. Every Valiant EHR user, and all the patients they assist, now benefit from predictions and guidelines that appear automatically on screen right when needed and in context. This includes the predictive analytics and toolkits from the “Heart Health Association”, which are deployed in the Valiant EHR and being used to help improve the health of thousands of people every day.

BENEFIT FROM KNOWLEDGE

Apply the health advice to real-world decisions for better outcomes.

Charles is a primary care physician responsible for taking care of a large group of patients. For years, he has worked to keep up with the latest medical evidence, but that is hard to do. Charles’ patients struggle with a diversity of health issues. The Knowledge Grid is now helping him keep up. Thanks to the Knowledge Grid, the "Valiant" EHR software Charles uses in the clinic provides his with useful medical advice in many disease areas, and the advice is tailored to each patient. Charles’ EHR performs both automated health data analysis to identify hidden patterns, as well as ad-hoc information requests from him. For example, it uses the "Heart Health Association" predictive analytics to determine which of my patients are at more risk of myocardial infarction and which would benefit most from surgery. Thanks to the Knowledge Grid, the new EHR significantly expands his abilities to not only improve patient outcomes but also to cut costs from unnecessary interventions.
